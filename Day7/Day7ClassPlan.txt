Day 7: Searching, Sorting & Space Complexity (6-hour online class)

Session goal:
Master fundamental searching and sorting algorithms. Understand the space complexity concept to evaluate algorithm efficiency beyond just time execution.

Class structure:

1. Welcome and Review (30 minutes)
- Recap Day 6 topics.
- Explain today's focus: Space Complexity, Searching, and Sorting elements in a collection.

2. Introduction to Space Complexity (45 minutes)
- Concept: Measuring memory usage of an algorithm relative to input size.
- Auxiliary Space vs Input Space.
- Examples of O(1) space and O(N) space.
- The trade-off between Time and Space complexity.

3. Binary Search Algorithm (60 minutes)
- The core requirement: Data MUST be sorted.
- The divide and conquer approach: High, Low, and Mid pointers.
- Visualizing the halving process.
- Time Complexity: O(log N) and Space Complexity: O(1) iterative vs O(log N) recursive.

Break (15 minutes)

4. Bubble Sort (60 minutes)
- Concept: Repeatedly swapping adjacent elements if they are in the wrong order.
- Time Complexity: O(N^2) in all cases (unless optimized). Space Complexity: O(1).
- Visualizing the largest element bubbling up to the end.
- Coding standard Bubble Sort.

5. Selection Sort (60 minutes)
- Concept: Finding the minimum element from the unsorted part and putting it at the beginning.
- Time Complexity: O(N^2) in all cases. Space Complexity: O(1).
- Coding Selection Sort.
- Comparing Selection Sort with Bubble Sort (fewer swaps).

Break (15 minutes)

6. Insertion Sort (45 minutes)
- Concept: Building a sorted array one element at a time, comparing backwards and shifting.
- Time Complexity: Best case O(N), Worst case O(N^2). Space Complexity: O(1).
- Why Insertion Sort is efficient for small or nearly sorted datasets.
- Coding Insertion Sort.

7. Review, Q&A, and Homework (30 minutes)
- Comparing the three O(N^2) sorting algorithms.
- Answer student questions on Space vs Time Complexity.
- Assign homework: 100 Practice Problems and 100 Interview Q&A.
