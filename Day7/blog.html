Space Complexity

While Time Complexity answers "How fast does it run?", Space Complexity answers "How much extra memory does it need?". We often measure auxiliary space (extra space beyond the input). All three sorting algorithms learned today operate in-place, meaning they have a space complexity of O(1).

Sorting Algorithms

We covered three classic O(N^2) sorting algorithms:

  • Bubble Sort: Repeatedly swaps adjacent elements. Simple but slow.
  • Selection Sort: Finds the minimum element and places it at the start. Minimizes swaps compared to Bubble Sort.
  • Insertion Sort: Builds a sorted array one element at a time. Very efficient for small or nearly sorted arrays.
