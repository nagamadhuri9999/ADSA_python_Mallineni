Day 8: Mastering Advanced Sorting Algorithms

Welcome to the summary of Day 8! Today, we leveled up our algorithmic skills by diving into the Divide and Conquer paradigm, exploring some of the most efficient sorting algorithms used in computer science: Merge Sort and Quick Sort.

The Divide and Conquer Paradigm

Divide and Conquer is a strategy that breaks a large problem into smaller, manageable subproblems, solves them recursively, and then combines the results.

Merge Sort vs. Quick Sort

  • Merge Sort: Consistently operates in O(N log N) time. It is a stable sort but requires O(N) auxiliary space because of the merging step. It's the go-to algorithm when stability is required or when sorting linked lists.
  • Quick Sort: Often the fastest algorithm in practice for sorting arrays in memory. It operates in O(N log N) average time and requires only O(log N) space. However, it is an unstable sort and can degrade to O(N^2) if a poor pivot is chosen.
